KENYA LIVESTOCK DISEASE GUIDE - CATTLE, GOATS, SHEEP, POULTRY
Source: KALRO, Ministry of Agriculture, GALVmed Kenya Survey

=== CATTLE DISEASES ===

1. EAST COAST FEVER (ECF / Ndigana Kali) - MOST IMPORTANT IN KENYA
Cause: Theileria parva parasite, spread by brown ear tick
Symptoms:
- High fever (above 40°C)
- Swollen lymph nodes especially behind ear and in groin
- Watery eyes, nasal discharge
- Difficulty breathing, animal stands with legs apart
- Death within 2-3 weeks if untreated
Treatment:
- Buparvaquone (Butalex) injection - MUST be given by vet EARLY
- Cost: around KSh 1,500-3,000 per treatment
Prevention:
- Immunization: ITM (Infection and Treatment Method) - once in lifetime
- Regular tick control: dip or spray every 7-14 days
- Acaricide: Triatix, Diazinon, Bayticol
- Check cattle DAILY for ticks especially in ears

2. FOOT AND MOUTH DISEASE (FMD)
Cause: Highly contagious virus
Symptoms:
- Blisters on mouth, tongue, feet, teats
- Excessive salivation, drooling
- Limping, reluctance to walk
- Drop in milk production
Treatment:
- NO CURE - supportive treatment only
- Isolate ALL infected animals IMMEDIATELY
- Report to veterinary officer - it is a notifiable disease
- Antibiotics to prevent secondary infections
Prevention:
- Vaccination every 6 months
- Do not move animals from infected areas

3. LUMPY SKIN DISEASE
Cause: Lumpy Skin Disease Virus, spread by insects
Symptoms:
- Raised nodules (lumps) on skin all over body
- Fever, nasal discharge
- Reduced milk production
- Swollen lymph nodes
Treatment:
- No specific treatment
- Anti-inflammatory drugs for fever
- Antibiotics to prevent secondary infections
Prevention:
- Vaccination every year
- Control biting insects (flies, mosquitoes)

4. BRUCELLOSIS (Ondoa Mimba)
Cause: Brucella abortus bacteria
Symptoms:
- Abortion in last 3 months of pregnancy
- Retained placenta
- Swollen joints in bulls
- Humans can get infected (fever, joint pain)
Treatment:
- No treatment - cull infected animals
- Do NOT drink raw milk from infected cows
Prevention:
- Vaccinate female calves at 3-8 months (RB51 or S19 vaccine)
- Test bulls before buying
- Do not introduce new animals without testing

5. CONTAGIOUS BOVINE PLEUROPNEUMONIA (CBPP / Ndigana)
Cause: Mycoplasma mycoides bacteria
Symptoms:
- Painful breathing, coughing
- Animal stands with legs apart, head down
- Fever
- Death in severe cases
Treatment:
- Oxytetracycline injection - only slows disease
- Report to vet - notifiable disease
Prevention:
- Vaccination every year
- Do not mix herds without quarantine

6. ANTHRAX (Sotoka)
Cause: Bacillus anthracis bacteria
Symptoms:
- SUDDEN DEATH with no prior symptoms
- Blood from body openings after death
- DO NOT OPEN CARCASS - spreads spores
Treatment:
- Penicillin if caught very early
- Usually no time to treat - death is rapid
Prevention:
- Vaccination every year (April-May before rains)
- BURN or deeply bury carcasses - do NOT skin or eat

7. BLACKQUARTER (Kimeta)
Cause: Clostridium chauvoei bacteria
Affects: Young cattle 6-24 months, well-fed animals
Symptoms:
- Sudden lameness, swelling in shoulder/hindquarter
- Crackling sound when pressing swelling
- Death within 12-36 hours
Treatment:
- High dose penicillin very early - often too late
Prevention:
- Vaccination every year
- Most common after start of rains

8. MASTITIS (Maziwa Mabaya)
Cause: Various bacteria infecting udder
Symptoms:
- Hot, swollen, painful udder
- Watery, stringy, or bloody milk
- Cow reluctant to be milked
Treatment:
- Intramammary antibiotic tubes (Mastijet, Orbenin)
- Insert tube after milking
- Milk affected quarter completely first
Prevention:
- Clean milking hands and equipment
- Dip teats in disinfectant after milking
- Dry cow therapy at end of lactation

=== GOAT AND SHEEP DISEASES ===

1. CONTAGIOUS CAPRINE PLEUROPNEUMONIA (CCPP) - MOST IMPORTANT
Cause: Mycoplasma capricolum bacteria
Symptoms:
- Coughing, difficulty breathing
- Fever, nasal discharge
- Animal stands apart from herd
- High mortality if untreated
Treatment:
- Oxytetracycline injection early
- Tylosin injection also effective
Prevention:
- Vaccination every year
- Quarantine new animals for 3 weeks before mixing

2. PPR (Peste des Petits Ruminants / Sotoka ya Mbuzi)
Cause: Morbillivirus - similar to cattle rinderpest
Symptoms:
- High fever, depression
- Discharge from eyes and nose
- Sores in mouth, diarrhea
- Deaths in 5-10 days
Treatment:
- No cure - supportive treatment
- Antibiotics to prevent secondary infection
Prevention:
- Vaccination every 3 years (provides long immunity)

3. MANGE (Upele)
Cause: Sarcoptes scabiei mites
Symptoms:
- Intense scratching
- Hair/wool loss starting from face, ears, legs
- Thick crusty skin
Treatment:
- Ivermectin injection (0.2mg/kg) OR
- Dipping in Diazinon/Amitraz solution
- Repeat after 14 days
Prevention:
- Quarantine new animals
- Dip regularly

4. INTESTINAL WORMS (Minyoo)
Cause: Various helminths (roundworms, tapeworms)
Symptoms:
- Weight loss despite eating
- Bottle jaw (fluid under chin)
- Pale gums (anemia)
- Pot belly in young animals
Treatment:
- Albendazole (Valbazen): 7.5mg/kg
- Ivermectin: also controls external parasites
- Fenbendazole (Panacur)
Prevention:
- Deworm every 3 months
- Rotate grazing areas
- Check FAMACHA eye score to decide when to deworm

=== POULTRY DISEASES ===

1. NEWCASTLE DISEASE (Mdondo) - MOST IMPORTANT
Cause: Paramyxovirus
Symptoms:
- Twisted neck, circling
- Gasping, rattling breathing
- Green watery diarrhea
- Sudden deaths in large numbers
Treatment:
- NO TREATMENT - vaccination ONLY prevents it
- Cull infected birds humanely
- Do NOT eat sick birds
Prevention:
- Vaccine every 3 months (I-2 thermostable vaccine)
- Can give via eye drop, drinking water, or spray
- Cheap: around KSh 50-100 for 100-dose vial
- MOST IMPORTANT VACCINE FOR KENYAN FARMERS

2. GUMBORO DISEASE (Infectious Bursal Disease - IBD)
Cause: Birnavirus
Affects: Chicks 3-6 weeks old
Symptoms:
- Ruffled feathers, droopiness
- White watery diarrhea
- Chicks peck at their own vent
Treatment:
- No treatment - supportive care (vitamins, electrolytes)
Prevention:
- Vaccine at day 14 and day 21 of life

3. FOWL TYPHOID & SALMONELLA
Cause: Salmonella gallinarum bacteria
Symptoms:
- Yellow-green diarrhea
- Sudden deaths especially in young birds
- Pale combs and wattles
Treatment:
- Enrofloxacin (Baytril) in water for 5 days
Prevention:
- Fowl Typhoid vaccine
- Keep feeders and drinkers clean
- Rodent control (rats spread Salmonella)

4. COCCIDIOSIS
Cause: Eimeria protozoa
Affects: Chicks 3-8 weeks
Symptoms:
- Bloody diarrhea
- Huddling, ruffled feathers
- High mortality in young chicks
Treatment:
- Amprolium (Coxitabs) in water
- Sulphonamides
Prevention:
- Coccidiostats in feed
- Dry clean litter
- Do not overcrowd

5. FOWL POX
Cause: Avipoxvirus
Symptoms:
- Wart-like scabs on comb, wattles, face (dry form)
- Yellow lesions in mouth and throat (wet form)
Treatment:
- No treatment for virus
- Iodine on scabs to prevent secondary infection
Prevention:
- Fowl Pox vaccine at 6-8 weeks

=== VACCINATION SCHEDULE SUMMARY ===

CATTLE:
- FMD: Every 6 months
- Anthrax: Every year (April-May)
- Blackquarter: Every year
- CBPP: Every year
- ECF: Once in lifetime (ITM method)
- Brucellosis: Females once at 3-8 months

GOATS/SHEEP:
- PPR: Every 3 years
- CCPP: Every year
- Anthrax: Every year in endemic areas

POULTRY:
- Newcastle Disease: Day 1 (day-old chick), week 3, then every 3 months
- Gumboro: Day 14 and Day 21
- Fowl Typhoid: Week 6

=== SIGNS OF HEALTHY LIVESTOCK ===
CATTLE:
- Alert and curious
- Normal temperature 38-39.5°C
- Bright eyes, moist nose
- Shiny coat
- Eating and ruminating
- Normal dung (firm, not runny)
- Producing normal milk

CHICKENS:
- Active, scratching and foraging
- Bright red comb and wattles
- Clear eyes
- Eating and drinking normally
- Firm droppings (white with dark center)
