KENYA MAIZE PRODUCTION - COMPLETE GUIDE
Source: KALRO, Ministry of Agriculture Kenya

=== VARIETIES BY ZONE ===

HIGHLAND ZONES (above 1800m) - Kiambu, Nyeri, Nyandarua, Nakuru:
- H614D: Most popular, disease tolerant, 120-140 days to maturity
- DK8031: High yield, tolerant to Gray Leaf Spot
- SC403: Good for highland areas
- WH507: Widely adapted variety

MIDLAND ZONES (1200-1800m) - Machakos, Kitui, parts of Rift Valley:
- H513: Medium maturity, good yield
- DK777: High yield, medium altitude
- WH507: Widely adapted

LOWLAND/DRY ZONES (below 1200m) - Coast, Ukambani, ASAL areas:
- WEMA (Water Efficient Maize for Africa): Most drought tolerant
- Duma 43: Short season 90 days, drought tolerant
- H614D: Also performs well in lowlands
- WH403: Suited for dry lowlands

=== PLANTING CALENDAR ===

Central Kenya (Kiambu, Nyeri, Murang'a, Kirinyaga):
- Long rains: February - March
- Short rains: October - November

Rift Valley (Nakuru, Nandi, Uasin Gishu):
- Long rains: March - April
- Short rains: August - September (some areas)

Western Kenya (Kakamega, Bungoma, Trans Nzoia):
- Long rains: March - April
- Short rains: August - September

Eastern Kenya (Machakos, Kitui, Meru):
- Long rains: March - April (plant immediately at first rains)
- Short rains: October (very important to plant early)

Coast (Kilifi, Kwale, Taita Taveta):
- Long rains: March - April
- Short rains: October - November

RULE: Plant within 2-3 days of first reliable rains.

=== LAND PREPARATION ===
- Plough to 20-25cm depth
- Harrow to break clods
- Make furrows or planting holes
- Row spacing: 75cm between rows
- Plant spacing: 25-30cm between plants
- Population: 53,000 plants per hectare

=== FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS (KALRO Official) ===

PLANTING FERTILIZER:
Option 1 (Best): DAP 50kg per acre (2.5 bags per hectare)
Option 2: MAP 46kg per acre
Option 3: NPK 23:23:0 - 2 bags per acre
- Place fertilizer in planting hole, cover with thin soil before placing seed
- NEVER let seed touch fertilizer directly

TOPDRESSING (3-4 weeks after planting):
Option 1 (Cheapest): CAN 50kg per acre (3 bags per hectare)
Option 2: Urea 25kg per acre (cheaper but needs care)
- Apply when soil is moist
- Place fertilizer 5cm from stem, not directly on plant

COMBINED ORGANIC + INORGANIC (best for soil health):
- 5 tons FYM/compost + 1.25 bags DAP + 1.5 bags CAN per hectare
- OR 10 tons FYM/compost alone per hectare
- Organic matter improves soil structure and water holding

=== WEED CONTROL ===
- First weeding: 2-3 weeks after planting (CRITICAL)
- Second weeding: 6-7 weeks after planting
- Herbicide: Atrazine (pre-emergence), apply before weeds appear
- Hand weeding also effective for small farms

=== MAJOR DISEASES ===

1. GRAY LEAF SPOT (Madoa ya Kijivu)
Caused by: Cercospora zeae-maydis fungus
Symptoms: Rectangular gray-brown spots on leaves, spots merge
Favored by: Cool humid weather, dense planting
Control: 
- Mancozeb or Chlorothalonil spray
- Plant resistant varieties: DK8031, H614D
- Crop rotation
- Remove crop residues after harvest

2. NORTHERN LEAF BLIGHT
Caused by: Exserohilum turcicum fungus
Symptoms: Long tan-colored lesions on leaves, cigar-shaped
Control:
- Propiconazole fungicide spray
- Plant resistant varieties
- Improve air circulation

3. MAIZE STREAK VIRUS
Caused by: Virus spread by leafhoppers (tiny insects)
Symptoms: Yellow streaks on leaves, stunted growth
Control:
- NO CURE - prevention only
- Imidacloprid seed treatment or spray to kill leafhoppers
- Plant resistant varieties
- Remove infected plants immediately

4. MAIZE LETHAL NECROSIS (MLN) - SERIOUS IN KENYA
Caused by: Two viruses together (MCMV + SCMV)
Symptoms: Yellowing from leaf tips, premature death, no cobs
Found in: Rift Valley, Western Kenya, Eastern Kenya
Control:
- Plant MLN tolerant varieties: SEEDCO SC403, DK8031 new versions
- Remove infected plants, burn them
- Control aphids and thrips (vectors) with Dimethoate
- Do not plant ratoon crops in affected areas

5. STALK ROTS
Caused by: Fusarium and Diplodia fungi
Symptoms: Stem rot from base, plant falls over before harvest
Control:
- Avoid waterlogging
- Balanced fertilization (don't over-apply nitrogen)
- Plant resistant varieties

=== PESTS ===

FALL ARMYWORM (Spodoptera frugiperda) - MAJOR PEST SINCE 2016
Symptoms: Window paning on leaves, holes in whorl, sawdust-like frass
Control:
- Spray at first sign: Coragen (Chlorantraniliprole) - most effective
- Ampligo, Voliam Targo also effective
- Traditional: Ash or sand in whorl of young plants
- Biological: Trichogramma parasitic wasps

STALK BORER (Busseola fusca) - Traditional pest
Symptoms: Dead heart in young plants, holes in stem
Control:
- Permethrin dust 2.5kg per hectare at 4-5 weeks
- Dipterex at 4kg per hectare
- Spray into whorl at first sign

APHIDS
Symptoms: Colonies on leaves, sticky honeydew, yellowing
Control: Dimethoate or Imidacloprid spray

=== HARVESTING & STORAGE ===
- Harvest when husks are brown and dry
- Moisture should be below 13% for safe storage
- Dry on raised platforms, not on ground
- Use hermetic storage bags (PICS bags) to prevent weevils
- Actellic Super dust for storage protection
- Expected yields with good management: 5-7 tons per hectare
