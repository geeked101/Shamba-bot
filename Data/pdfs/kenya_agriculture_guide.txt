KENYA GENERAL AGRICULTURE GUIDE
Source: Ministry of Agriculture Kenya & KALRO

=== MAIZE PRODUCTION IN KENYA ===

IMPORTANCE:
Maize is the staple food crop of Kenya, grown by over 75% of smallholder farmers.
Annual production: 3-4 million metric tons
Main growing regions: Rift Valley, Western, Nyanza, Central

SEED SELECTION:
Certified hybrid seeds give 2-3 times more yield than local varieties.
Recommended varieties by zone:
- Highland (above 1800m): H614D, DK8031, SC403
- Midland (1200-1800m): WH507, H513, DK777
- Lowland (below 1200m): H614D, WH403, Duma 43
- Drought prone areas: WEMA varieties, DH04

LAND PREPARATION:
- Plough to depth of 20-25cm
- Harrow to break clods
- Make furrows or holes 75cm between rows, 25cm between plants

PLANTING:
- Plant 2 seeds per hole, thin to 1 after germination
- Depth: 5-7cm
- Population: 53,000 plants per hectare

WEED CONTROL:
- First weeding: 2-3 weeks after planting
- Second weeding: 6-7 weeks after planting
- Herbicides: Atrazine (pre-emergence), Primagram Gold

HARVESTING:
- Harvest when husks are dry and brown
- Moisture content should be below 13% for storage
- Average yield: 2-3 tons/ha (smallholder), up to 8 tons/ha (commercial)

=== POTATO PRODUCTION ===

VARIETIES:
- Dutch Robjin: Most popular, good yield, moderate disease resistance
- Kenya Mpya: Bred for Kenya, good blight resistance
- Sherekea: High yield, suitable for chips
- Shangi: Good for boiling and chips

SEED:
- Always use certified seed potato
- Seed rate: 1,500-2,000kg per hectare
- Chit seed (sprout) before planting for uniform emergence

PLANTING:
- Ridge and furrow system
- Row spacing: 75cm, plant spacing: 30cm
- Planting depth: 10-15cm

DISEASE MANAGEMENT:
Late blight (most serious):
- Spray preventively before disease appears
- Ridomil Gold MZ, Mancozeb, Dithane M45
- Spray every 7-10 days during wet season

=== HORTICULTURE - TOMATOES ===

VARIETIES:
- Anna F1: Very popular, good yield, virus resistant
- Rambo F1: Large fruits, good shelf life
- Tylka F1: Early maturing, suitable for highlands
- Money Maker: Open pollinated, good for processing

NURSERY:
- Raise seedlings in nursery for 4-6 weeks
- Treat nursery soil with Dithane before planting
- Harden seedlings before transplanting

TRANSPLANTING:
- Transplant at 4-6 leaf stage
- Spacing: 60cm x 60cm
- Water immediately after transplanting

STAKING:
- Stake indeterminate varieties at 30cm height
- Use sisal string or wire for support

COMMON PESTS:
1. Tomato Leafminer (Tuta absoluta) - very destructive
   Control: Coragen, Ampligo, pheromone traps
2. Whitefly - spreads viruses
   Control: Confidor, Actara
3. Spider Mites - yellowing and webbing
   Control: Dynamec, Abamectin

=== SOIL FERTILITY MANAGEMENT ===

SOIL pH:
- Most crops prefer pH 5.5-7.0
- Below 5.5: Apply agricultural lime
- Apply lime 2-3 months before planting
- Rate: 2-4 tons per hectare depending on soil test

INTEGRATED SOIL FERTILITY MANAGEMENT (ISFM):
Combine:
1. Mineral fertilizers (DAP, CAN, NPK)
2. Organic matter (compost, manure, crop residues)
3. Improved varieties
4. Good agronomic practices

Benefits:
- Reduces fertilizer cost by 30-50%
- Improves soil structure
- Increases water holding capacity
- Sustainable long-term productivity

COMPOSTING:
Materials: Crop residues, kitchen waste, manure, grass
Process:
1. Layer materials in 1m x 1m x 1m heap
2. Keep moist but not waterlogged
3. Turn every 2 weeks
4. Ready in 2-3 months
5. Apply 5 tons per hectare

=== CLIMATE SMART AGRICULTURE ===

DROUGHT MANAGEMENT:
- Plant drought tolerant varieties
- Mulching reduces water loss by 60%
- Tied ridges harvest rainwater
- Zai pits capture rainwater for dry areas

CONSERVATION AGRICULTURE:
- Minimum tillage - disturb soil as little as possible
- Permanent soil cover - mulch or cover crops
- Crop rotation - rotate cereals with legumes

Benefits of crop rotation:
- Breaks pest and disease cycles
- Improves soil fertility (legumes fix nitrogen)
- Reduces fertilizer costs
- Increases yields
