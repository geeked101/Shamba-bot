KENYA COFFEE AND TEA FARMING GUIDE
Source: Kenya Coffee Board & Tea Board of Kenya

=== COFFEE FARMING IN KENYA ===

SUITABLE AREAS:
Kenya's coffee is among the world's finest - Arabica variety grown in highlands.
Best altitudes: 1,500 - 2,100 meters above sea level
Key counties: Kiambu, Nyeri, Muranga, Kirinyaga, Embu, Meru, Kisii

PLANTING:
- Plant seedlings at start of long rains (April-May) or short rains (October-November)
- Spacing: 2.7m x 2.7m (1,370 trees per hectare)
- Dig holes 60cm x 60cm x 60cm
- Add 2 debe of well-rotted manure per hole

FERTILIZER SCHEDULE:
Young trees (year 1-2):
- Apply CAN at 100g per tree, 4 times per year
- Apply during March, June, September, December

Mature bearing trees:
- After harvest: NPK 17:17:17 at 200g per tree
- February: CAN at 150g per tree  
- May: CAN at 150g per tree
- August: Sulfate of Potash at 100g per tree

COFFEE BERRY DISEASE (CBD) MANAGEMENT:
This is the most serious disease of Kenya coffee.
Spray schedule with copper fungicide:
- First spray: When 50% of trees are in flower
- Second spray: 6 weeks after first spray
- Third spray: 6 weeks after second spray
- Fourth spray: When berries are turning red

HARVESTING:
- Only pick fully red ripe cherries
- Selective picking every 1-2 weeks
- Main crop: October to January
- Fly crop: April to June
- Average yield: 600-900kg clean coffee per hectare

PRUNING:
- Annual pruning is essential
- Use single stem system for most varieties
- Prune after main harvest (January-February)
- Remove dead, diseased and crossing branches

=== TEA FARMING IN KENYA ===

SUITABLE AREAS:
Kenya is the world's third largest tea producer.
Best altitudes: 1,500 - 2,700 meters above sea level
Key counties: Kericho, Nandi, Bomet, Nyamira, Kisii, Murang'a

PLANTING:
- Plant during long rains for best establishment
- Spacing: 1.2m x 0.75m in double rows
- Use certified cuttings from registered nurseries
- Mulch heavily after planting

HARVESTING (PLUCKING):
- Pluck every 7-14 days during growing season
- Pluck 2 leaves and a bud (fine plucking)
- Avoid coarse plucking - reduces quality
- Annual yield: 2,000 - 4,000kg made tea per hectare

FERTILIZER FOR TEA:
Apply 4 times per year:
- NPK 25:5:5 at 250kg per hectare each application
- Total annual: 1,000kg NPK per hectare
- Apply dolomitic limestone if soil pH below 4.5
- Tea prefers slightly acidic soil (pH 4.5-5.5)

BLISTER BLIGHT MANAGEMENT:
Most serious disease of tea in Kenya highlands.
- Apply copper oxychloride spray every 7-10 days during wet season
- Reduce shade trees to improve air circulation
- Avoid plucking when foliage is wet

STEM CANKER:
- Remove affected branches immediately
- Paint cut surfaces with copper paste
- Improve drainage in affected areas

=== SOIL HEALTH FOR BOTH CROPS ===

Soil testing:
- Test soil every 3 years at KALRO laboratories
- Cost: KSh 500 per sample
- Contact: KALRO Headquarters, Nairobi Tel: 020 4183301

Signs of poor soil health:
- Yellowing leaves despite fertilizer application
- Poor growth and low yields
- Increased disease incidence
- Soil becoming hard and cracking

Improving soil health:
- Add organic matter (compost, manure) annually
- Practice minimal tillage
- Maintain ground cover to prevent erosion
- Plant cover crops in bare areas
