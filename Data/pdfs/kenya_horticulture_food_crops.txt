KENYA HORTICULTURE AND FOOD CROPS GUIDE
Source: KALRO, Ministry of Agriculture Kenya

=== BEANS (Maharagwe) ===

RECOMMENDED VARIETIES (KALRO):
- Nyota: Most popular, drought tolerant, 1000-1600m altitude
- Rose Coco (Mwitemania): Popular in Central Kenya, red mottled
- Canadian Wonder: Large seeds, good for export
- KK8: Disease tolerant, good for Western Kenya
- Wairimu: High yield, medium altitude
- Lyamungu 85: Good for highland areas

PLANTING:
- Plant at onset of rains
- When intercropped with maize: plant soon after maize
- Spacing: 45cm x 15cm (pure stand)
- Depth: 3-5cm
- 2 seeds per hole

FERTILIZER:
- Beans fix their own nitrogen through root bacteria
- Apply Rhizobium inoculant on seeds before planting
- If soil is poor: DAP 25kg per acre at planting only
- Do NOT topdress beans with nitrogen - causes excess leaves, few pods

WEED CONTROL:
- First weeding: 2 weeks after emergence
- Second weeding: before flowering
- Do NOT weed during flowering - causes flower drop

DISEASES:
Bean Rust (Kutu):
- Orange powder on underside of leaves
- Spray Mancozeb or Zineb
- Plant resistant varieties

Angular Leaf Spot:
- Angular brown spots on leaves following leaf veins
- Spray copper fungicide
- Use certified disease-free seed

Bean Common Mosaic Virus:
- Yellow mosaic pattern on leaves, distorted growth
- No cure - remove infected plants
- Use certified virus-tested seed
- Control aphids with Dimethoate

STORAGE:
- Dry to below 12% moisture
- Use hermetic PICS bags to prevent bean weevils
- Actellic Super dust (2g per 1kg beans) for storage

=== POTATOES (Viazi) ===

MAJOR GROWING AREAS:
- Nyandarua (most important), Meru, Nakuru, Kericho, Elgeyo-Marakwet

VARIETIES:
- Dutch Robjin: Most widely grown, round-oval, good yield
- Kenya Mpya: Bred by KALRO, blight tolerant, oval shape
- Sherekea: High yield, good for chips, oblong
- Shangi: Good flavour, round, late maturing
- Desiree: Red skin, good for boiling

SEED POTATOES:
- ALWAYS use certified seed - reject sprouted or diseased seed
- Seed rate: 1,500-2,000kg per hectare
- Chitting (pre-sprouting): Place in light, cool area for 4-6 weeks before planting
- Chitted seed gives more uniform emergence and 20% higher yield

PLANTING:
- Plant in ridges and furrows
- Row spacing: 75cm between rows
- Plant spacing: 30cm between plants
- Planting depth: 10-15cm
- Apply manure in furrow before planting: 5 tons per hectare

FERTILIZER:
- At planting: DAP 100kg per acre
- Topdressing at earthing up: CAN 50kg per acre
- Potassium is important for tuber quality: use NPK 17:17:17 in low K soils

EARTHING UP:
- Cover plants with soil when they reach 20-25cm height
- Prevents greening of tubers (solanine - toxic)
- Improves drainage, increases yield

LATE BLIGHT (Ugonjwa Mkubwa wa Viazi) - MOST SERIOUS DISEASE:
- Caused by Phytophthora infestans
- Symptoms: Water-soaked spots on leaves turning brown-black, white mold underneath, rotten smell
- Spreads rapidly in cool, wet weather
- SPRAY PREVENTIVELY before disease appears:
  Week 1-4: Ridomil Gold MZ (systemic) every 7-10 days
  After week 4: Alternate Ridomil with Mancozeb
- Fungicide resistance: Do not use Ridomil alone for whole season
- Remove and bury infected plants

EARLY BLIGHT:
- Small brown spots with yellow rings (target board appearance)
- Spray Mancozeb or Dithane M45

HARVESTING:
- Harvest when 80% of vines are yellow/dead
- Let potatoes dry in field for 2-3 days before sacking
- Handle gently - bruises cause rotting
- Store in cool, dark, ventilated store (not in plastic bags)

=== TOMATOES (Nyanya) ===

VARIETIES:
- Anna F1: Most popular, virus resistant (TYLCV), good yield
- Rambo F1: Large fruits, good shelf life for transport
- Tylka F1: Early maturing, suitable highlands
- Kilele F1: Good for Rift Valley areas
- Money Maker: Open pollinated (cheaper seed), good for processing

NURSERY:
- Raise seedlings for 4-6 weeks
- Treat nursery soil with Dithane before sowing
- Sow thinly: 1g of seed per square meter
- Water regularly in morning only
- Harden before transplanting: reduce watering last week

TRANSPLANTING:
- Transplant at 4-6 leaf stage (4-6 weeks old)
- Spacing: 60cm x 60cm
- Water immediately after transplanting
- Transplant in evening or on cloudy day

STAKING:
- Stake indeterminate varieties when 30cm tall
- Use sisal string or wire
- Prune to 2 main stems for best yield

FERTILIZER:
- Basal: DAP 50kg per acre at transplanting
- Every 2 weeks: CAN 25kg per acre
- Calcium foliar spray: prevents blossom end rot
- Potassium: Sulfate of potash at fruiting stage improves quality

MAJOR DISEASES:
Bacterial Wilt:
- Plant wilts suddenly despite water
- Cut stem - brown ring inside, slimy ooze
- No cure - remove plant, do NOT replant tomatoes in that spot for 3 years
- Use resistant varieties

Early and Late Blight:
- Brown spots on leaves (early blight: concentric rings)
- Spray Ridomil Gold, Mancozeb, Dithane M45

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV):
- Leaves curl upward, yellow margins
- Stunted plants, no fruit
- Spread by whiteflies
- Use TYLCV-resistant varieties (Anna F1, Rambo F1)
- Control whiteflies with Confidor or Actara

MAJOR PESTS:
Tuta absoluta (Tomato Leafminer):
- Most destructive pest in Kenya since 2014
- Mines in leaves, attacks fruits
- Spray: Coragen (Chlorantraniliprole) most effective
- Ampligo, Voliam Targo also work
- Use pheromone traps to monitor

Whitefly:
- Tiny white insects under leaves
- Spread viruses, cause yellowing
- Spray: Confidor (Imidacloprid), Actara (Thiamethoxam)

Spider Mites:
- Fine webbing under leaves, yellow stippling
- Spray: Dynamec, Abamectin
- Increase humidity (mites like dry conditions)

=== CABBAGE AND KALE (Sukuma Wiki) ===

VARIETIES:
Kale: Thousand Headed (most popular), Collards
Cabbage: Gloria F1, Pruktor F1, Star 3301

FERTILIZER:
- At transplanting: DAP 50kg per acre
- Every 3 weeks: CAN 50kg per acre (kale needs lots of nitrogen)

MAJOR PESTS:
Diamondback Moth (most serious):
- Small holes in leaves, leaves eaten from below
- Hard to control due to resistance
- Spray: Coragen, Dipel (Bt - biological), Emamectin benzoate
- Rotate chemicals to prevent resistance

Aphids:
- Colonies under leaves, curled leaves
- Dimethoate, Actara spray

Cutworms:
- Seedlings cut at ground level
- Diazinon in soil at planting

=== AVOCADO (Parachichi) ===

GROWING AREAS: Central Kenya, Rift Valley highlands

VARIETIES:
- Hass: Export quality, pebbly black skin when ripe
- Fuerte: Smooth green skin, popular locally
- Jumbo: Large fruits, local market

PLANTING:
- Use grafted seedlings from certified nurseries
- Spacing: 6m x 6m
- Dig hole 60x60x60cm with manure

FERTILIZER: NPK 17:17:17 twice per year, CAN for young trees

ANTHRACNOSE (most serious disease):
- Black spots on fruits, fruits rot
- Copper fungicide spray during wet season

=== MANGO (Embe) ===

GROWING AREAS: Coastal, Eastern, Western Kenya

VARIETIES:
- Apple Mango: Most popular in Kenya
- Kent, Keitt: Export varieties
- Tommy Atkins: Good shelf life for export

FRUIT FLY - Major pest:
- Fruits fall before ripening
- Methyl eugenol traps to capture males
- Protein bait sprays
- Harvest at right maturity

POWDERY MILDEW:
- White powder on flowers and young fruits
- Spray sulfur or systemic fungicide at flowering
